History:

The Mandala Monument was built in 1994 and was completed in 1996 to commemorate the services of a hero in freeing West Irian from the hands of invaders as well as a reward for the services of the second former Indonesian President, Suharto. The former Indonesian second president was also Commander of the Mandala Command who was instrumental in regulating a strategy to liberate West Irian. As is known, even though it had proclaimed independence for almost 20 years, the Dutch still controlled West Irian. Since the liberation operation was successful, West Irian returned to the bosom of the motherland.

The liberation operation of West Irian began on January 2, 1962 with the issuance of a presidential decree on the establishment of the Mandala Command and ten days after President Soekarno appointed Maj. Gen. Suharto as Commander of the Mandala and Makassar to be designated as the center of the Mandala Command Headquarters. From this headquarters located on Jalan Jenderal Sudirman, operations to seize West Irian were controlled so that the Indonesian government finally became fully sovereign over West Irian since 1 May 1963.

reason for being famous:.

Unlike the 3rd floor, on the 3rd floor we can see a replica of the Mandala Commander's office, complete with maps of West Irian, photos of preparations for the departure of troops, posts and clothing used during the Mandala operation. Entering the 4th floor is a viewing room where visitors can see the atmosphere of the city of Makassar from a height.

On the outer wall of the monument is realized the flames that symbolize the surge of enthusiasm to liberate West Irian, while in the body of this monument there are reliefs that tell the history of the struggle for the liberation of West Irian.
