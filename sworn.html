Jawaban:

The equivalents of those words in Indonesian language are as follows

- descent (noun) - penurunan

- a sick leave (noun) - cuti sakit

- reacquaint (verb) - berteman kembali

- settle in (verb) - menetap, tinggal

- marque (noun) - merek/label

- respectively (adverb) - secara berurutan

- retirement (noun) - pensiun

- resignation (noun) - pengunduran diri

- sworn in (verb) disumpah

- relinquish (verb) - menyerahkan/ meninggalkan

- release (verb) menerbitkan

Penjelasan:

In matching the proper English words with the provided translated words, you need to see them carefully. Some words are quite easy to determine but some others can be tricky. The easy way can be done by looking up to the dictionary. Yet, you you also use common sense to guess the possible meaning like 'sick leave' or 'resignation'. When it comes to part of speech, such has to be written properly. The 'verb' is ideally written without -ed or -ing, but only V1 form. Thus, they should be 'reacquaint', 'settle in', 'relinquish', just like 'release', but the 'sworn in' is more like past participle so it has to be translated as passive.

There is one mistake in one of the word in the exercise above. The word 'descent' should be 'penurunan', not 'keturunan'. The right translation for 'keturunan' is 'descendant' (noun).
